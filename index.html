Women's Water Polo

Welcome

UTS is very proud of it's women's Water Polo program. Over the last few years with lots of hard work, many successful results have been achieved. Players have reached the international level. The team also brought home the gold medal at the 2017 Australian Uni games championship held on the Gold Coast. As a result, this team was awarded "team of the year" at the 2017 UTS Sport Blues Awards.

Continue through this website to see more specific inofrmation of the team and results of games and don't forget to have a look at our social media pages.

2017

Team of the year:

Recap

In 2017 the women's water polo team attended the Australian University Games championships and went through the enitre competition undefeated and won the gold medal. The grand final was against UNSW and UTS came out on top 3-2. This was UTS's first gold medal in this event for the women. This was also player, Sam El Safty's 8th University Games so ending it on a gold medal win was very rewarding for her. This team's success was awarded at the UTS Sport Blues Awards night as they recieved the "Team of the Year" award.

Recap

In 2018 the women's team were favourites to go back to back as they had maintained majority of the same players from last year. The competion for the women started off well as they won their first three games quite convincingly. Their last round game was against Queensland University of Technology who were very strong. UTS ended up loosing this game 6-4. UTS still had a chance to make the grandfinal as long as they won their next two games. The Quater Final was against Sydney Uni where UTS won. The Semi Final was then against ACU. UTS were down 4-1 at half time and managed to get the game back to 5 all. However, ACU managed to score another goal in the dying seconds to make the final score 6-5. This was a letdown to the team but they look ahead into ensuring they secured the bronze medal. The bronze medal game was against UNSW which was a rematch of the 2017 Gold medal game. UTS remained on top for entirety of the game taking home the bronze medal 10-5.
